Ultimate Ascent 2013

The Centaur of Gravitar

The 2013 robot challenge was termed ultimate ascent, a game revolving around scaling pyramids and shooting goals. Points were granted to shooting frisbees through goals or limbing the tower, the former being very easy to exploit. However, due to the previous year's experience with shooting mechanisms and our relative disinterest with a repeat of mechanics, hardships, toil, and exploding commodes, we decided to instead create a mobile climber. Due to its mechanical complexity, it suffered for a portion of the Portland Reigional but successfully scaled the pyramid during the Spokane Reigional and was able to complete the 50 point climb. This was met with great enthusiasm by Amitha. Due to our team efforst expent on the design of the robot, we recieved the Xerox Creativity Award and earned us the highest ranking our team has attained so far, 9th place.

The Centaur of Gravitar made use of two three modules: the climbing arms, drivetrain, and frisbee dump. Our robot would first make use of its relatively weak drivetrain to make a beeline for the frisbee loading station. Once in position, one of the human drivers would manually load the dumping mechanism and insure that all frisbee were in the correct orientation. Then, under the careful direction of Erin Feebeck, the robot would maneuver itself beneath the first rung of the pyramid where it would use its three arms to begin the ascent. Once at the last rung, the bucket would extand outwards and hinge to dump the payload into the top receptable. Sometimes in practice our drivetrain would prove to be a tad slow, or our dumper a bit too exubrant, losing some points from our 50 point cap. However, our entire team still remained pourd of our centaur, who had managed to capture the show the rounds it completed.
